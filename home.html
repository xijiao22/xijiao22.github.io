Who Am I

I am a Professor and past chair of the Cognitive Science department at UCSD, where I have been working since the department was created in 1989. My research interests vary, but all turn on understanding how we are tightly coupled to the outside world, and how that interactive coupling has shaped the inner and outer workings of our cognitive skills and capacities. Over the years I have looked at how the environment can be configured to simplify and extend cognition, including how we intelligently use space to cope with the complexity of everyday life, and how we use external representations as an interactive tool for thought. Most recently, I have been studying the ways humans use their bodies as things to think with, specifically in dance making and choreographic cognition. My approach combines theory, experiment and ethnographic study. Because my focus is on how we interact with the environment I am sensitive to the relevance of cognitive science for design. As that field expands, and as the digital increasingly interpenetrates the physical, I think the importance of our field will grow and we should be self-conscious about our influence in that expanding area.

Currently Working On

Three big ideas have been occupying my time recently: how to speed up thought, how we think in different sensory modalities - thinking with our bodies is just one of these, and what it means for something to be a coordinating structure and why it's at the very heart of what distributed cognition is all about.
